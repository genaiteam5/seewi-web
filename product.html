Overview

Composed of AL6061 aluminium, carbon laminate, OLED micro-display
Breath-based feedback in lieu of score rings
Designed and assembled in Seoul

Description

Minimal yet considered, SEEWI reimagines the discipline of Gukgung as a precise digital practice. A sensor-laden bow reads draw length and tension, a pair of monochrome glasses overlays a breathing horizon, and a near-invisible finger sensor captures the exact instant of release. The system translates posture, focus, and breath into a quiet measurement — never a score — so that the archer's attention stays with the body. Built for ritual as much as for training, SEEWI invites stillness, repetition, and a slow return to the centred shot.

Smart Bow

A precision-instrumented Gukgung. Sensors built into the grip and limbs read the archer's draw with the same care a master takes when nocking the arrow.

Draw tracking
Measures draw length to a tenth of an inch as the string travels.
Tension sensing
Reads applied force across the limbs and surfaces it through a quiet signal.
Release timing
Detects the instant the string is released, correlated against breath.
02 / 03 — Smart Glasses

Smart Glasses

A monochrome HUD designed to disappear. Breath rhythm, focal alignment, and aim assist surface only when needed — then return to stillness.

Breathing HUD
A faint horizon line rises and falls with the wearer's breath.
Focus guide
A subtle reticle indicates focal stability over the last six seconds.
Aiming assistance
Minimal red crosshair, opacity tuned to ambient light.

Tracking Device

A near-invisible finger sensor reads the release the way the body experiences it — not as a frame, but as a moment.

Finger tracking
Three-axis motion across the draw hand at 1 kHz sampling.
Motion analysis
Detects micro-corrections in the seconds before release.
Release detection
Captures the precise moment of release within ± 1 ms.
